Cleanliness Comparison: New York City vs. New Delhi

Disgustometer Scale: 1 = Cleanest, 10 = Most Disgusting/Worst Imaginable

Dimension NYC
(Disgustometer Score)		 New Delhi
(Disgustometer Score)
Visible Waste
(trash, litter, animal waste)		 4/10[1]
NYC has frequent trash collection and street sweeping; ~95% of streets are rated “acceptably clean,” but curbside garbage bags and some litter remain. 		7/10[2]
Delhi sees large amounts of uncollected trash, open dumping, and animal waste. Stray dogs, cattle, and insufficient trash pickups lead to visibly dirtier streets.
Microbial Contamination
(bacteria, pathogens on surfaces)		 6/10[3]
NYC sidewalks often test high for fecal bacteria (from dog waste and rats). Although sanitation is relatively good, hidden germs are common. 		9/10[4]
Open sewage, garbage piles, and frequent presence of fecal matter make Delhi’s street surfaces extremely prone to harmful bacteria and parasites.
Odors
(garbage, sewage, street smells)		 5/10[5]
NYC can smell strongly of garbage, especially in summer or near trash bags. However, odors are usually intermittent or localized. 		8/10[6]
Delhi has pervasive smells from trash dumps, sewage, open drains, and decomposing waste, creating more constant, intense odors.
Air Quality
(pollution, smog, particulate matter)		 4/10[7]
NYC’s air is moderate compared to many large metros; PM2.5 levels generally meet US standards except during unusual events (e.g., wildfire smoke). 		10/10[8]
Delhi consistently ranks among the world’s most polluted cities. Dense smog and very high PM2.5/PM10 levels make it extremely hazardous.
Street Cleaning Efficiency
(frequency & effectiveness of sanitation)		 3/10[9]
NYC has one of the world’s largest sanitation departments, frequent street sweeping (daily to weekly), and robust waste collection schedules. 		7/10[10]
Delhi has insufficient equipment & manpower. Many streets are swept infrequently; waste collection is inconsistent, resulting in significant backlog.
Public Perception of Cleanliness
(opinions of residents & visitors)		 6/10[11]
Many New Yorkers complain of dirty streets and rats; surveys rank NYC among “dirty” global cities, though some areas are notably cleaner. 		8/10[12]
Residents and tourists often describe Delhi as very unclean; polls regularly place it near the bottom among major Indian cities for sanitation.
Shoe-Bottom Contamination
(likelihood of picking up filth & germs)		 7/10[13]
NYC sidewalks harbor fecal bacteria, spilled food, and street grime. Shoe soles typically test positive for multiple pathogens—disgusting but not the worst. 		10/10[14]
Delhi’s streets often have open waste, sewage, and animal feces, so shoes get heavily contaminated. High risk of bacteria, parasites, and offensive filth.
